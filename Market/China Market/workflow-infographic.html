Act as a senior U.S. equity market strategist, macro analyst, sector rotation researcher, technical analyst, ETF strategist, and institutional market commentator.
Today is [Today's Date].
I am a macro trader and ETF investor. I mainly focus on:
Major U.S. indexes: S&P 500, Nasdaq 100, Dow Jones, Russell 2000
Sector rotation
Market breadth
Money flows and liquidity
Interest rates and bond yields
Fed expectations
Earnings and guidance
AI / technology leadership
Risk appetite
Technical levels
ETF positioning and trading implications
Please produce a comprehensive Daily U.S. Market Recap and Analysis based on the latest available market data after today's close.
Use reliable sources where possible, such as Reuters, Bloomberg, CNBC, WSJ, MarketWatch, FactSet, S&P Dow Jones Indices, Nasdaq, NYSE, Federal Reserve, U.S. Treasury, CME FedWatch, FRED, and company earnings releases.

In addition to the written report, create a self-contained interactive HTML page based on the report.
The HTML must:
- be a single standalone file with all CSS and JavaScript inline
- not rely on external build steps, frameworks, or local data files
- present the key conclusions, market data, sector rotation, macro drivers, ETF implications, and outlook in an interactive dashboard or report format
- include clear section navigation and at least a few interactive elements such as tabs, filters, toggles, accordions, or scenario switches
- be readable on both desktop and mobile
- clearly label any data that was unavailable or not independently verified rather than inventing values

Daily U.S. Market Recap and Analysis

1. Executive Summary
Start with 8–12 key bullet points answering:
- Was today a risk-on or risk-off session?
- Which major indexes led or lagged?
- Was the move broad-based or narrow?
- Which sectors led and lagged?
- What were the main catalysts?
- Did rates, the dollar, oil, or volatility affect equities?
- Was today's market action bullish, bearish, or mixed?
- What is the key takeaway for a macro trader / ETF investor?

2. Major Index Performance
Analyze today's performance of: S&P 500 / SPY / VOO / IVV, Nasdaq 100 / QQQ, Dow Jones / DIA, Russell 2000 / IWM.
For each index include: daily % change, closing level, intraday high/low if relevant, whether it closed near the high or low of the day, YTD performance if useful, relative strength vs other indexes, key technical levels (20/50/100/200-day MAs), support and resistance. Explain what the index action says about market sentiment.

3. Market Breadth and Internal Strength
Advancers vs decliners; new highs vs new lows; % of stocks above 20/50/200-day MAs; equal-weight vs cap-weight S&P 500; small caps vs large caps; Nasdaq breadth; sector participation.
Answer: Was today's move driven by a few mega-cap stocks or broad participation? Is breadth improving or deteriorating? Does breadth confirm or contradict the index move?

4. Sector Rotation
Analyze all 11 S&P 500 sectors: Technology, Communication Services, Consumer Discretionary, Consumer Staples, Health Care, Financials, Industrials, Energy, Materials, Utilities, Real Estate.
For each: daily performance, relative strength/weakness, key drivers, whether the move looks defensive/cyclical/growth-led/value-led, ETF implications (XLK, XLC, XLY, XLP, XLV, XLF, XLI, XLE, XLB, XLU, XLRE). Identify strongest and weakest sectors of the day.

5. Key Stock and Theme Movers
Magnificent 7: AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, AMZN, GOOGL, META, TSLA; Semiconductors: NVDA, AMD, AVGO, MU, INTC, SOXX, SMH; AI infrastructure and cloud; banks and financials; energy/oil-related; consumer; defensives; major earnings movers; major news-driven movers. Explain whether leadership is healthy or concentrated.

6. Macro Drivers
Treasury yields (2y/10y/30y); yield curve movement; U.S. dollar index; oil; gold; Bitcoin/crypto if relevant; credit spreads if available; VIX and volatility structure. Explain how these affected equities today.

7. Federal Reserve and Rate Expectations
Fed speeches/comments; economic data affecting rate expectations; CME FedWatch implied probabilities; expectations for next Fed meeting; whether data supports cuts/hikes/longer pause; impact on growth, value, bonds, small caps. Explain whether the market is pricing a soft landing, stagflation, recession, or renewed inflation.

8. Economic Data and News Catalysts
CPI/PCE/PPI; jobs data; jobless claims; retail sales; ISM/PMI; consumer confidence; GDP; housing; Treasury auctions; geopolitical; trade/tariff; fiscal/policy. For each: actual result, consensus, market reaction, investment implication.

9. Earnings and Corporate News
Major companies reporting; EPS and revenue beats/misses; guidance changes; margin trends; analyst reactions; sector-level earnings implications; impact on index direction. Explain whether earnings are supporting or weakening the trend.

10. Money Flows, Liquidity, and Positioning
ETF flows; equity fund flows; money market fund assets; Treasury liquidity; Fed balance sheet/QT; options positioning; put/call ratio; dealer gamma/expiry effects; short interest/crowded positioning. Explain whether flows and positioning are supportive or risky.

11. Technical Analysis
For S&P 500, Nasdaq 100, Russell 2000, 10-year yield, U.S. dollar index, VIX: trend direction, support, resistance, moving averages, momentum, breakout/breakdown risks, overbought/oversold. Classify as Bullish / Neutral / Bearish / Improving / Deteriorating.

12. ETF Strategy Implications
SPY/VOO/IVV, QQQ, IWM, DIA, RSP, SMH/SOXX, XLK, XLF, XLE, XLU, TLT/IEF/SHY, GLD, Bitcoin ETFs. Which show relative strength; which are weakening; momentum vs defensive; which may be overextended; pullback opportunities.

13. Risk Assessment
Main risks for the next 1–5 trading days: inflation surprise, Fed repricing, earnings disappointment, overbought technicals, narrow leadership, liquidity stress, geopolitical risk, dollar strength, yield spike, oil shock, weak breadth. Rate short-term risk: Low / Moderate / Elevated / High. Explain why.

14. Market Outlook
Next trading day (key levels, catalysts, bullish/bearish scenarios); next 1–2 weeks (trend, sector leadership, macro risks, ETF positioning); next 1–3 months (regime, earnings trend, Fed policy impact, risk/reward for equities and bonds).

15. Actionable Takeaways for a Macro Trader / ETF Investor
What to watch tomorrow; what data matters next; sectors with momentum; defensive areas; aggressive/neutral/cautious; buy-the-dip vs reduce-risk; ETF watchlist.
Final conclusion format:
Market Bias: Bullish / Neutral / Bearish
Risk Appetite: Strong / Moderate / Weak
Leadership Quality: Broad / Narrow / Deteriorating
Best Opportunities: [list]
Main Risks: [list]
ETF Positioning View: [explain]

Style Requirements
Professional but practical tone. Avoid vague comments — use data, market levels, clear reasoning. When data is unavailable, say so clearly instead of guessing. Always distinguish short-term trading signal vs medium-term trend vs long-term implication. Goal: understand what happened today, why, and how to prepare for the next trading day.

HTML Deliverable Requirements
- After completing the report, generate the self-contained interactive HTML based on the same analysis
- Keep the design professional, information-dense, and easy to scan for a macro trader / ETF investor
- Prefer a polished dashboard or briefing-page layout over a plain article dump
- Use the same conclusions and data points as the written report so the HTML and report stay consistent
- Make the HTML useful as a standalone artifact that can be opened directly in a browser

PROMPT · 输入
▶ 查看完整提示词USD/CAD prompt 
Act as a professional FX strategist, macro trader, and technical analyst.
Create a complete USD/CAD currency pair recap and outlook for [Today's Date].
The goal is to help an investor or trader understand what drove USD/CAD, whether the move was USD-driven, CAD-driven, oil-driven, rate-driven, or risk-sentiment-driven, and how the setup affects short-term and medium-term positioning.
Please structure the report as follows:

1. Executive Summary
Provide a concise summary covering:
- Whether USD/CAD moved higher, lower, or stayed range-bound
- Whether the move was mainly driven by USD strength/weakness or CAD strength/weakness
- The most important macro driver of the period
- Whether the market tone was risk-on, risk-off, defensive, commodity-led, or rate-driven
- Key technical levels traders should watch next
Clearly state: "The USD/CAD move was mainly driven by ______."

2. Price Action Recap
- Opening level, high, low, and latest/closing level
- Daily/weekly/monthly percentage change
- Intraday behavior if relevant
- Whether USD/CAD closed near the high, low, or middle of the range
- Whether the move showed trend continuation, reversal, breakout, failed breakout, or consolidation
Include a short interpretation of what the price action suggests.

3. Fundamental Drivers
U.S. Dollar Side: U.S. economic data (CPI, PCE, payrolls, jobless claims, retail sales, ISM, GDP); Fed expectations; U.S. Treasury yields (2y/10y); DXY; risk sentiment and safe-haven demand; U.S. political/fiscal headlines. Explain whether the USD was supported or pressured.
Canadian Dollar Side: Bank of Canada policy expectations; Canadian CPI, GDP, employment, retail sales, housing, business surveys; Canada-U.S. yield spread; Canadian trade balance and terms of trade; domestic political/fiscal headlines. Explain whether CAD was supported or pressured.

4. Oil and Commodity Influence
WTI crude; Brent if relevant; natural gas if relevant; broader commodity sentiment; whether oil strength supported CAD or oil weakness pressured CAD; whether the CAD response to oil was strong, weak, or disconnected.
Clearly state: "Oil was a positive / negative / neutral factor for CAD because ______."

5. Interest Rate and Yield Spread Analysis
U.S. 2y vs Canada 2y; U.S. 10y vs Canada 10y; Fed vs BoC rate-cut/hike expectations; whether yield spreads favored USD or CAD; whether the move in USD/CAD matched the rate differential signal.
Explain: "The yield spread signal currently favors ______."

6. Risk Sentiment and Equity Market Impact
S&P 500 / Nasdaq risk appetite; VIX; credit spreads if relevant; global risk-on/off; whether CAD behaved like a pro-cyclical currency; whether USD gained safe-haven demand. Explain whether USD/CAD was moving as a risk pair or a rate-differential pair.

7. Technical Analysis
Daily chart: trend direction; 20/50/100/200-day MAs; price above/below key MAs; RSI (overbought/oversold/neutral); MACD; Bollinger Band position.
Weekly chart: broader trend structure; major support/resistance zones; long-term uptrend/downtrend/range; key breakout/breakdown levels.
Key levels: immediate resistance; major resistance; immediate support; major support; bullish breakout level; bearish breakdown level.
Format:
Level | Importance | Interpretation
[price] | Major resistance | Break above may signal USD/CAD upside continuation
[price] | Support | Break below may signal CAD strength

8. Scenario Analysis
Bullish USD/CAD: stronger USD, higher U.S. yields, Fed more hawkish than BoC, weak oil, risk-off, weak Canadian data — include upside targets.
Bearish USD/CAD: weaker USD, lower U.S. yields, BoC more hawkish than Fed, strong oil, risk-on, strong Canadian data — include downside targets.
Range-Bound: mixed U.S./Canadian data, stable oil, no major change in yield spreads, balanced risk sentiment — include range support and resistance.

9. Trading and Hedging Implications
For a trader/investor holding USD and exposed to CAD risk: whether conditions favor holding USD, reducing USD exposure, or gradually hedging; whether USD/CAD is near an attractive hedge level; whether to use spot conversion, limit orders, staged conversion, or options; the technical level that confirms a stronger CAD move; the level that confirms further USD strength.
Framework:
USD/CAD Condition | Suggested Action
Breaks above resistance | Delay CAD conversion / keep more USD exposure
Fails at resistance | Start partial USD-to-CAD hedge
Breaks below support | Increase CAD hedge
Remains range-bound | Use staged conversion

10. Key Upcoming Catalysts
U.S. CPI/PCE; U.S. jobs report; Fed meeting or speeches; Canada CPI; Canada employment report; Bank of Canada meeting; oil inventory data; OPEC headlines; U.S. Treasury auctions; major risk events. Explain which catalyst matters most for USD/CAD.

11. Final View
Final Bias: Neutral / Bullish USD/CAD / Bearish USD/CAD
Main Driver: [Fed-BoC spread / oil / risk sentiment / USD trend]
Key Support: [level]
Key Resistance: [level]
Confirmation Signal: [price or macro trigger]
Invalidation Risk: [what would make the view wrong]

Extra Instruction
Use current market data, cite reliable sources, and separate confirmed facts from interpretation. Avoid overconfidence. If data is mixed, clearly say the setup is mixed.
