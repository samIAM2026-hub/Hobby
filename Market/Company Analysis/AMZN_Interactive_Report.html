Investment Thesis synthesized from all sources

Amazon enters mid-2026 with its fastest AWS growth in 15 quarters (+28% y/y, $150B run-rate), a record 13.1% operating margin, and an AI order book that includes a $364B AWS backlog plus $100B+ decade-long commitments from both Anthropic and OpenAI. The retail engine keeps expanding margins through automation and same-day logistics, while advertising (~50%+ estimated EBITDA margins per CFRA) compounds at 20%+. The trade-off: a planned ~$200B capex year pushes free cash flow negative in 2026, debt has nearly doubled to $119B, and insiders have sold heavily. Every fundamental analyst in this packet still lands bullish — CFRA Strong Buy ($325), Argus Buy ($325), Morningstar 4-star ($280 FVE, wide moat) — with monetization of AI investments expected in 2027–2028.

Verdict: BUY / ACCUMULATE — 12-month target zone $280–325

Weight of evidence: 5 of 6 rating sources are outright bullish; the most cautious (Morningstar Quant) still sees the stock slightly below fair value. The Street's mean target of $308.77 implies ~26% upside, and estimate momentum is strongly positive (FY26 consensus EPS +13% in 90 days).

Why now: Growth is re-accelerating across every segment, margins are at records, and the forward P/E (29×) sits at roughly half its 5-year average while EPS compounds ~20–25% — a PEG of ~1.2–1.4×, cheaper than the mega-cap peer set on growth-adjusted terms.

How: Per Argus, establish or dollar-average into positions, using weakness (the stock is −10% over the past month) as entry. Suitable for long-term growth allocations; expect volatility around the Oct 2026 FTC trial and quarterly capex headlines.

What would change the thesis: AWS growth decelerating below ~20% while capex stays at $200B; FCF still deeply negative into 2027; an adverse FTC outcome forcing structural remedies; or AI customers (OpenAI/Anthropic) failing to honor compute commitments.
